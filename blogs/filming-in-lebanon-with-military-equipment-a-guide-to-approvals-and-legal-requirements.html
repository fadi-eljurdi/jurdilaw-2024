FILMING IN LEBANON WITH MILITARY EQUIPMENT: A GUIDE TO APPROVALS, AND LEGAL REQUIREMENTS

Lebanon's captivating landscapes, historical architecture, and vibrant culture have long made it a sought-after destination for international and local filmmakers alike. Whether you're shooting an action-packed thriller or a historical drama, filming in Lebanon often involves complex logistics?especially when incorporating military gear or choreographed stunts. This article outlines the procedures, legal requirements, and safety measures filmmakers need to consider when planning a shoot involving the use of military equipment in filming in Lebanon.


A. Military Gear and Stunts: What the Law Requires


The use of military gear in filming is tightly regulated under Lebanese law to ensure both public safety and national security. Productions seeking to utilize weapons, military vehicles, or even military personnel must apply for official clearance through the Lebanese Armed Forces. This process, though manageable, is detail-intensive and time-sensitive.


B. Application Procedure: How to Get Started


To secure filming approvals in Lebanon for military content, a formal request must be submitted to the LAF. The application for filming using military/army assets should include:


  1. Beneficiary: Full legal name of the production company or individual responsible.
  2. Purpose of Filming: Clear description of the project (film, series, documentary, commercial, etc.).
  3. Filming Details: Exact shooting locations, and specified dates and times.
  4. Military Assistance Needed: List of personnel (e.g., advisors or actors), as well as specific equipment (vehicles, firearms, uniforms).
  5. Detailed Schedule: A scene-by-scene breakdown indicating when and where military elements will be used.
  6. Supporting Documents: Scripts, storyboards, production plans.
  7. Contact Information: Updated address, email, and phone numbers.


Approvals for stunt clearances in Lebanon must also be included in this process if stunts involve high-risk elements or interactions with military equipment.

It is also worth noting that filming using drones will also require clearance from the Lebanese arm and the submission of a special drone filming form.


C. Legal and Safety Considerations


The LAF reserves the right to approve or deny any application, and processing can take several weeks. It's essential to allow for this timeline in your production schedule. In addition:


  1. Security Restrictions: Some areas may be off-limits due to national security concerns.
  2. Costs: Filmmakers may be liable for associated costs, including transportation and deployment of military personnel and gear.


Productions are also expected to adhere to stringent filming safety in Lebanon standards. This includes complying with industry best practices for safety measures on set, particularly when executing stunts or using pyrotechnics.


D. Stunt Approvals and On-Set Safety


Securing stunt approvals involves working with both the LAF and local safety inspectors. This ensures that all high-risk sequences are carried out under expert supervision. Utilizing licensed stunt coordinators and having emergency medical services on standby are part of the best practices enforced.


Moreover, producers should conduct a comprehensive risk assessment and implement proper insurance coverage to protect crew, cast, and the general public.


E. Final Thoughts: Compliance and Creativity


Navigating Lebanese law on military use and stunts may seem daunting, but with careful planning and adherence to legal protocols, filmmakers can bring their creative visions to life in this stunning Mediterranean locale. Before proceeding, always consult with Lebanese government offices or legal professionals to stay current on regulations.
