How to Start Business Partnerships in Lebanon (2025 Guide)

Lebanon has long been recognized for its skilled workforce and open economic policies.

Despite facing recent challenges, the country still serves as an attractive gateway to the Middle East for international investors looking to form business partnerships in Lebanon.

Over the years, Lebanon has launched multiple legislative and administrative reforms to attract foreign investment and improve its business climate.


 

Key Takeaways


  • Lebanon offers various legal structures for business partnerships and foreign entities.
  • IDAL (Investment Development Authority of Lebanon) supports foreign investors with incentives and streamlined processes.
  • Joint Stock Companies and Limited Liability Companies are the most commonly used legal structures.
  • Foreign ownership is allowed in most sectors, except in sensitive industries like media and defense.
  • Business models like branch offices and representative offices are also available with specific operational rules.


 

Business Partnerships in Lebanon: Why Legal Structure Matters


For both foreign and local businesses, selecting the correct legal framework is the first step toward a successful entry into the Lebanese market.

Each model has different requirements for ownership, governance, permits, and operation.

Understanding these legal options is critical to forming strong and sustainable business partnerships in Lebanon.


 

History of Business Law & Investment Incentives in Lebanon


From the early 1990s until 2004, Lebanese authorities implemented various economic reforms to stimulate foreign direct investment (FDI).

In 2001, Investment Law No. 360 was passed to offer tax exemptions, and a faster licensing process.

These benefits are coordinated through IDAL, which was established in 1994 to promote investment in the country.

This law allows both local and foreign businesses to set up Joint Stock Companies and Limited Liability Companies, with very few restrictions on foreign ownership (outside of strategic sectors).


 

Common Legal Structures for Business Partnerships in Lebanon


Investors can choose from several legal forms when establishing a presence in Lebanon.

Below are the most commonly used models:

 

Joint Stock Company (Soci?t? Anonyme Libanaise - SAL)


  • Governed by Decree No. 304 of 1942.
  • Suitable for large-scale enterprises.
  • Must appoint at least three shareholders.
  • Requires a minimum capital investment.
  • Needs a board of directors and legal representation.
  • No restriction on foreign ownership (except in sensitive sectors).
  • Legal requirement: Annual lawyer retainer.

 

Limited Liability Company (Soci?t? ? Responsabilit? Limit?e - SARL)


  • Regulated by Decree No. 34 of 1967.
  • Suitable for small to medium enterprises (SMEs).
  • Requires a minimum of one partner and capital contribution.
  • Fewer corporate formalities than Joint Stock Companies.
  • Also requires a local legal advisor on retainer.


Both SAL and SARL allow foreign ownership and offer flexibility for international investors.

 

Branch of a Foreign Company


Foreign companies can set up a branch office to conduct general commercial activities.


Permits required:


  • Ministry of Economy and Trade
  • Ministry of Labor


In some cases, you?ll need residency permits through the Directorate General of General Security.

Visit the Ministry of Economy website for more details on licensing.

 

Representative Office in Lebanon


A representative office can only perform non-commercial activities, such as:


  • Promoting the parent company
  • Marketing products or services


It cannot directly engage in sales or revenue-generating activities.

This is ideal for early-stage market exploration or brand development.

Why Choose Business Partnerships in Lebanon?


Even with economic instability, Lebanon offers strong advantages for foreign entities:
