If you are wondering whether there is a framework to crowdfund a movie production business in Lebanon, the answer is, in principle, yes! and here is how:


On June 11, 2013 the Financial Market Authority / Central Bank Governor and in implementation of the law 161/2011, issued a decision in relation to crowdfunding in Lebanon, through which startups, small and medium companies in lebanon can seek to fund their operations through crowdfunding by offering stock or shares for subscription. 


According to the referenced decision, crowdfunding can only be conducted through a) a licensed Lebanese organisations formed as a joint stock companies and whose main objective is to crowdfund, or b) foreign crowdfunding companies through a branch duly incorporated in lebanon with an operating capital of 1 Billion USD (then ~670,000 USD and currently with the depreciation of the Lebanese Lira ~ 11,000 USD). Following licensing, a crowdfunding company should start its operations within a maximum period of 6 months from licensing, under the pain of revoking the license granted. 


Crowd funding organisations are prohibited from a) providing consultancy services to its clients, b) receiving deposits or offering through an electronic platform any bonds or financial tools other than shares and stocks, as well as trading (directly or indirectly) the stocks and shares booked for crowdfunding.

Companies applying for crowdfunding should provide: 


  • A list of the names, surnames and nationalities of the chairman and board members for that current year, major shareholders, the assistant general manager to the Chairman, directors and assistant directors. 
  • A criminal record dating back no more than three months, for each of the board members, managers, and commissioned auditors.
  • Company bylaws and the organizational structure (the bylaws of the foreign ?company? duly certified).
  • A statement of registration with the commercial registry and a copy of its bylaws as duly registered (for a ?foreign company?, a duly certified certificate of registration in the country of origin).
  • An audited financial statement (if available). 
  • A feasibility study covering a period of three years and includes a detailed statement of the projected profits and losses, the expected budgets, and the projected cash flow.
  • A statement indicating whether the ?funding mobilization? is carried out by subscribing to an SPV.
  • SPV bylaws.
  • A true copy of a statement issued by the competent authorities in the country of origin, stating that the SPV has obtained the required license.
  • An agreement to be signed with the investors that includes all details related to the conditions for subscribing to stocks or shares.
  • A term sheet study to be presented to the ?investors? at the full responsibility of the company and includes at least the following information:
  • the capital required to be mobilized and the number of ?shares or shares? offered for subscription.
  • the basic investment conditions, especially the minimum ?investment?, specifying whether the investment will be made through direct subscription or through a special purpose vehicle.
  • A risk assessment.
  • An explicit statement clarifying that neither the crowdfunding institution nor the Financial Market Authority has any relationship with the content of the term sheet study. 


If approved and successful, the crowdfund will be deposited in an escrow account with one of the banks operating in Lebanon until the stocks or shares purchase is affected in accordance with the agreement between the investors and the company seeking the funding. The fund may not remain in the escrow account for more than one hundred and eighty days, during which an actual subscription to the stocks or shares should take place, provided that the value of the capital required is fully funded, otherwise the money will be refunded to the subscribing investors immediately in addition to applicable interest (unless interest is forfeited by agreement of concerned parties).
