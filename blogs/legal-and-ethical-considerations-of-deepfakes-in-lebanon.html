Legal and Ethical Considerations of Deepfakes in Lebanon


Deepfake technologies have fundamentally changed how creators produce and consumers engage with digital media.

These AI-powered tools can manipulate voices, facial features, and entire identities?blurring the line between reality and fabrication.

Developers must pay close attention to the legal implications of deepfake.


Understanding the Legal Framework in Lebanon


In Lebanon, developers of deepfake tools  are not operating in a legal vacuum.Two main legal references govern the intersection of technology and accountability:


  1. Law 81/2018?The Electronic Transactions and Personal Data Law
  2. The Lebanese Penal Code


Although Law 81/2018 does not explicitly reference ?deepfakes,? it provides the necessary legal foundation to prosecute misuse.

Under this law, using personal and biometric data without permission can lead to legal action. This includes biometric identifiers like facial recognition and voice data.

Meanwhile, provisions from the Lebanese Penal Code address broader offenses, including identity theft, defamation, fraud, and obstruction of justice.

This is the core framework Lebanese lawyers use to assess liability in deepfake-related cases.


When Developers Become Legally Liable


Developers are not shielded from liability. In reality, Lebanese courts may hold developers accountable under several legal theories, including


  1. Accomplice Liability
  2. Instigation of Crime
  3. Accessory Conduct


A developer can face prosecution if they:


  1. Encourage someone to misuse the tool.
  2. Knowingly assist in fraudulent activity.
  3. Offer material or technical support with malicious intent.


For example, a developer creating a face-swapping app knowing that it will be used to impersonate a political figure to manipulate elections or cause political turmoil in the country could face criminal liability.

This is why developers should consult trusted legal counsel early in the development process.


Certain Legal Offenses Triggered by Deepfake Technologies


The misuse of deepfake technologies in Lebanon (and probably elsewhere) can expose both developers and end users to a broad range of legal liabilities. While public attention often focuses on the creators of deepfake tools, end users?those who generate, share, or circulate deepfake content?may also face prosecution, especially when their actions cause reputational damage, financial fraud, or violations of privacy rights.

In Lebanon, the primary legal instruments governing deepfake-related offenses are Law No. 81 of 2018 (Electronic Transactions and Personal Data) and relevant provisions of the Lebanese Penal Code.


Data Privacy Violations

Law 81/2018 prohibits the unauthorized collection, processing, or dissemination of personal data?including biometric identifiers such as facial images and voice patterns commonly used in deepfake apps. Developers who handle data without explicit user consent or fail to apply appropriate protective measures can face civil liability, regulatory fines, and enforcement actions. End users who upload or share manipulated content without permission may also be liable for violating personal data laws in Lebanon. 

Another area where deepfakes raise legal concerns is in cases of deception and impersonation.


Fraud and Identity Impersonation

Law 81/2018 also addresses digital fraud and impersonation. Developers and users alike may be prosecuted when deepfake tools are used for deception?such as scams, identity theft, or social engineering. Liability increases where developers do not take reasonable precautions against foreseeable misuse.


Defamation Using AI

Defamation is governed by the Lebanese Penal Code. Deepfakes that damage an individual's reputation?by mimicking their voice, image, or actions?can lead to criminal charges and financial damages. Both the creator and distributor may be held responsible.


Forgery and Obstruction of Justice

Using deepfakes to falsify documents or deceive authorities may constitute forgery or obstruction of justice under the Penal Code. These are serious offenses carrying penalties such as imprisonment or disqualification from professional practice. Lebanese law does not distinguish strictly between developers and users. Anyone who facilitates or enables harmful uses of deepfake technology may face civil or criminal consequences.


How the Foreseeability Test Applies


One key tool courts use to assess developer liability is the foreseeability test.


This legal standard asks:

  1. Was the developer aware the tool could be misused?
  2. Were disclaimers, technical safeguards, or policies implemented?
  3. Did the developer act once notified of abuse?


For example, if a developer gets a warning that someone is using their app for blackmail and does nothing, they might seem involved.

Legal services focused on digital compliance can help with risk management and policy implementation.


Practical Legal Strategies for Developers


To minimize exposure to litigation, developers should proactively incorporate ethical and legal considerations into their design process.


Recommended steps include


  1. Embedding watermarks or cryptographic identifiers into all generated media.
  2. Requiring explicit user consent before processing facial or vocal data.
  3. Including clear terms of service that prohibit malicious or deceptive uses.
  4. Maintaining detailed logs of user activities related to deepfake generation.
  5. Refusing service to clients engaging in fraudulent activity.
  6. Apply robust KYC and KYB measures.


Each of these practices can help demonstrate that the developer took reasonable precautions to avoid harm.


Consulting a qualified lawyer before launching deepfake-related software is strongly recommended to ensure compliance with Lebanese and other applicable laws.


International Perspectives and Future Regulation


Globally, countries are tightening regulations around AI-generated content.

The European Union has an AI Act. U.S. states have laws against unauthorized synthetic impersonation. The legal trend is clear: creators of these tools are taking more responsibility.

In Lebanon, similar reforms may follow.

This adds more pressure on law firms in Lebanon. They need to stay ahead and help clients with local rules and international standards.


Final Thoughts


Developers of deepfake technology are no longer passive players in the digital ecosystem.

Their tools have real-world consequences?from reputational harm to election interference and fraudulent activity.

The Lebanese legal system equips authorities to hold these developers accountable through criminal liability.

As courts improve how they handle digital harm, developers must understand their responsibilities and take proactive legal measures to ensure compliance. 

Developers navigating deepfake and AI-related technologies in Lebanon should remain informed about evolving legal obligations and risk management practices. Staying ahead of these issues is essential to responsible innovation within the bounds of applicable law.


FAQs


Can someone sue a developer in Lebanon if another person misuses their deepfake tool?

Yes. If the developer knowingly facilitates or fails to prevent foreseeable misuse, they can face liability as an accomplice or accessory.


What Laws Apply to Deepfake Misuse in Lebanon?

No specific law addresses deepfake explicitly. Law 81/2018 focuses on electronic transactions and data privacy, and the Lebanese Penal Code deals with fraud, impersonation, and tampering with evidence, which can be some of the classifications of malicious use of deepfake technology. 


How Developers Can Protect Themselves from Legal Risk

By embedding safeguards like watermarking, requiring consent, logging user activity, and consulting with legal counsel before releasing AI tools.


1. Watermarking AI Content

How it helps legally: Prevents misuse, forgery, and misinformation claims.

Example: A developer includes invisible digital watermarks in every AI-generated video. If someone uses the content maliciously, the developer can prove it was machine-generated and that steps were taken to signal this to users.

Why it protects developers: Shows that the developer took reasonable precautions, which can help reduce liability.


2. Requiring User Consent

How it helps legally: Ensures compliance with data privacy laws like Law 81/2018 (Lebanon), GDPR, or CCPA.

Example: An app requires users to opt-in before collecting biometric data like voice or face scans.

Why it protects developers: Having logged user consent can serve as a legal defense if there's a complaint or investigation.


3. Logging User Activity

How it helps legally: Creates an audit trail that proves how the software was used.

Example: A chatbot logs conversations securely. If someone claims it gave harmful advice, the logs can verify what really happened.

Why it protects developers: Demonstrates transparency and helps resolve disputes or legal claims effectively.


4. Consulting Legal Counsel From the Start

How it helps legally: identifies legal risks like bias, misuse, or regulatory non-compliance early in the process.

Example: A startup developing a deepfake generator consults a law firm to review their terms of use and data processing protocols.

Why it protects developers: Legal advice helps avoid major issues before launch and supports a defense if challenged later.


Are Deepfake Laws in Lebanon Up to Date?


While Lebanon lacks deepfake-specific laws, authorities use existing frameworks to regulate this technology. Future amendments are likely as AI use expands.
